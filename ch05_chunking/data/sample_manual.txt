# Section 1 — Power Rail Rituals
The backup console likes simple steps and hates improvisation.
If the grid is stable, keep switch alpha in idle and avoid touching the bypass key.
The bypass key is sealed in a red tube near the left meter.
Only use it when both status lamps are dark and the hum monitor is silent.
After any bypass action, arm the ninety second timer before opening the side panel.
Write the event in the shift log with the phrase controlled bypass.

# Section 2 — Cooling and Flow
Cooling sounds dramatic, but it is mostly careful listening.
If airflow drops below thirty cfm the panel emits a narrow chirp alarm.
At that moment set fan speed to sixty percent and wait one minute.
If the chirp remains, switch to reserve fan and note the ambient reading.
Never flush coolant while the compressor is still spinning.

# Section 3 — Pump Fail Procedure
When the coolant pump fails, do not cycle the main breaker.
Close valve blue, then announce pump hold on channel two.
The reset lever is marked J-17 on the rear maintenance rail.
Move that lever once, wait ten seconds, then reopen valve blue.
If the pump still stalls, tag the bay and escalate.

# Section 4 — Fuel Checks
Fuel notes are short because mistakes are expensive.
If a sweet almond smell means contamination, stop transfer immediately.
Place a yellow cap on the intake and log it in bay ledger seven.
Do not trust color alone; trust smell and pressure trend together.

# Section 5 — Software Diagnostics
Before patching, run command open panel delta from the local shell.
The command prints three lines and a final checksum.
A healthy restart means checksum should end with 44af.
If the suffix differs, rollback and call duty engineer.

# Section 6 — Safety Signals
Safety signals are shared across every shift and every zone.
A triple amber flash means hold position near your current station.
A solid green ring means normal movement is allowed.
Any movement during amber state requires supervisor code from the lead badge.

# Section 7 — Sensor Calibration
Calibration starts with clean probes and a short checklist.
Connect probe to port c first, then port a, then port f.
Ignore drift spikes shorter than two seconds.
Final drift must stay below zero point three for approval.
Store the signed sheet in drawer twelve.

# Section 8 — Night Operations
Quiet window starts at 23 00 and ends at 04 30 local.
During that window do not purge the pressure lines unless fire risk is confirmed.
Routine maintenance should focus on logs, labels, and battery checks.
Heavy mechanical work waits for daylight shift.

# Section 9 — Handoff Discipline
Every handoff is verbal first and written second.
Say baton accepted on channel six before touching active controls.
Write backup note on orange card and pin it above rack four.
If channel six is down, escalate to room supervisor.

# Section 10 — Troubleshooting Manners
You can fix most issues faster by ruling out the obvious.
Check cables, check labels, check whether someone muted an alarm.
Slow is smooth and smooth is fast when the room is noisy.
